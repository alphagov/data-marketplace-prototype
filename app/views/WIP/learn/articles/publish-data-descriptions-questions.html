Check questions for publishing data descriptions before you start

The Data Marketplace is a place for people who work in government organisations, agencies and public bodies to discover data available from other organisations and request access to it.

To allow your data to be discovered, it needs to have detailed descriptions that are understandable to a wide audience, not just those in your domain. This means you should avoid the use of acronyms, abbreviations and domain jargon.

The questions below are used to help you provide a description of your data. The questions differ slightly based on whether you’re adding a dataset or data service.

More information about what these questions mean will be given on the page as you go through the pages and add your description.

You will need to ensure your description meets data protection and data sharing standards.

Questions you will be asked

Dataset description

  • What is the title of the data?
  • Are there any alternative titles for the data?
  • What is the data about?
  • Please provide a short summary of the data.
  • What internal identifier do you have for the data?
  • What are the themes associated with the data?
  • What are the keywords associated with the data?
  • Who is the point of contact for this data?
  • Which organisation(s) have created the data?
  • What is the version number of the data?
  • When was the data issued?
  • When was the data last modified?
  • What licence will be used to allow the data to be shared?
  • What access rights does the user have?
  • What is the security classification of the data?
  • Are there any related data?
  • How is the data made available?
  • How often is the dataset updated?
  • What is the title of the distribution?
  • What is the download URL?
  • What is the file size?
