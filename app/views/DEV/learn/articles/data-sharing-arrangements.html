Set up a data share arrangement in government

Setting up a data share can take several months.

Step 1. Find the data you need

The Data Marketplace is a place for people who work in government organisations, agencies and public bodies to share data.

You can use it to search data sets from government organisations.

Find the data you need

Step 2. Submit a data share request

You need to answer some questions to make sure your request meets GDPR and data sharing standards.

Your answers will be reviewed by the department that owns the data.

Some questions may require support from people with skills in:

  • technology
  • data security
  • data protection
  • legal
  • data governance

Check questions before starting a data share request

Start data share request

Step 3. Collaborate with data owner

If your request is approved, you will need to provide additional information to the data owner.

What information the provider needs will vary slightly between organisations.

Step 4. Complete a memorandum of understanding (MOU)

An MOU is a statement of intent between two organisations. Your MOU will cover the details of your data sharing agreement.

Many organisations and departments have their own MOU templates.
